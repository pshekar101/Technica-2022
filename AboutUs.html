About Us

We are MyMentor, a mentorship service to help you connect with other people in LinkedIn. Our goal is to help immigrants move into different professional fields of work such as management, business, engineering, and more.

Our Story

The U.S. Census Bureau conducts surveys every year in order to record the number of foreign born and natural born citizens living in the country. Using the data collected by them, our team analyzed the different statistics and came to the conclusion that there are significantly more native born citizens working in technology and management related positions than immigrants.

Linear Regression Model

We are looking to build a generalized model in the form of y=mx+b, where b is the intercept and m is the slope of the line. Because we often don’t have enough information or data to know the exact equation that exists in the wild, we have to build this equation by generating estimates for both the slope and the intercept.

It is from this line above that we obtain our coefficients. Where the line meets the y-axis is our intercept (b) and the slope of the line is our m. Using the understanding we’ve gained so far, and the estimates for the coefficients provided in the output above, we can now build out the equation for our model.

In this case, the linear regression equation becomes y = 0.8933(x) + 2.8340, revealing how, on average, 2.83% of native born Americans work, and for every one percent increase in native born Americans working, there is a 0.8933% increase in foreign born Americans.

The stars, or "asteriks" near the Pr(> |t|) for foreign, reveals how there is some statistical significance for our model. Combined with the fact that the adjusted R squared value is 0.45, it can be said that there is definitely a correlation in the graph displayed.

The Q-Q plot on the left show how if both sets of quantiles came from the same distribution, we should see the points forming a line that's roughly straight, revealing how this is a normally distributed diagram. Normal distribution is good, because it indicates how the data is continuous and that there is a relationship present here.

What Does this Graphic Tell Us

From this graphic, one can easily identify how there are significantly more native born Americans working in professional or "white-collar" industries. These industries include jobs in management, finance, business, sales, etc. The graph also reveals how there are a higher proportion of foreign born individuals working in service based industries such as construction, maintenance, transportation etc.

As a result, after seeing how there was such a disparity in the percent of people working in different industries, we wanted to help immigrant populations gain an equal footing by offering a mentorship service for them. By taking a simple quiz, they can connect with people in similar fields to jumpstart their career in a professional field!
